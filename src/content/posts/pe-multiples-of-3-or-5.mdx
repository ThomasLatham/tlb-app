# Introduction

Last night, I decided to start a new hobby: Project Euler. More precisely, I'm setting out to solve
Project Euler problems and write about my attempts at doing so here. I had been missing mathematics
for some time, and I was in search for something I could program and also write about (and also
maybe distract me from the rest of my life at the moment). This first problem, though only rated 5%
difficulty by Project Euler, certainly gave me what I was looking for, and I hope you the reader
will enjoy reading about my thought process and solutions. So without further ado, let's get into
the problem!

# Problem Statement

**Multiples of 3 or 5:**

_If we list all the natural numbers below 10 that are multiples of 3 or 5, we get 3, 5, 6 and 9. The
sum of these multiples is 23. Find the sum of all the multiples of 3 or 5 below 1000._
